# Daily Nutrition & Fueling Strategy: 30k Trial Race

### 1. Daily Energetic Targets (Race Day)
- **Estimated TDEE**: ~3,016 kcal (This will be higher due to the race)
- **Macronutrient Target Split**:
  - **Carbohydrates**: 504g (focus on this)
  - **Protein**: 126g
  - **Fats**: 55g

### 2. Targeted Workout Fueling Timeline

- **Phase 0: The Day Before**
  - *Physiological Goal*: Maximize muscle glycogen stores (carb-loading).
  - *Action*: Your meals on September 5th should be high in easily digestible carbohydrates like pasta, rice, and potatoes, with moderate protein and low fat and fiber.

- **Phase 1: Pre-Workout (Race Morning, 2-3 hours before race start)**
  - *Physiological Goal*: Top off liver glycogen after an overnight fast.
  - *Target*: **84g of Carbohydrates**.
  - *Real-World Options*: A large bowl of oatmeal with a sliced banana and honey; or 2-3 slices of white toast/bagels with a thick layer of jam.

- **Phase 2: Intra-Workout (During the 30k Race)**
  - *Physiological Goal*: Sustain blood glucose levels, spare muscle glycogen, and delay fatigue.
  - *Target*: **60-90g of carbohydrates per hour** & **400-800ml of fluid per hour**.
  - *Real-World Options*: This translates to **one energy gel/chew every 20-25 minutes**. Start your first gel at the 30-minute mark and continue like clockwork. Alternate between water and sports drink at aid stations if available.

- **Phase 3: Post-Workout Recovery Window (Within 30-60 minutes after finishing)**
  - *Physiological Goal*: Rapidly replenish glycogen and kickstart muscle repair.
  - *Target*: **84g of Carbohydrates** and **25g of Protein**.
  - *Real-World Options*: A pre-made recovery shake is ideal. Other options include chocolate milk, or a simple meal like a chicken sandwich or rice bowl if available at the finish line.

### 3. Integrated Daily Meal Structure (Race Day)
- **Meal 1 (Pre-Race Breakfast)**: Your 84g carb meal 2-3 hours before the start.
- **Meal 2 (Intra-Race Fueling)**: Your 60-90g/hour gel/chew strategy.
- **Meal 3 (Post-Race Recovery Snack)**: Your 84g carb / 25g protein snack immediately after finishing.
- **Meal 4 (Post-Race Dinner)**: A large, satisfying meal rich in carbohydrates, protein, and micronutrients to continue the recovery process. Don't be afraid to eat a big meal; your body needs the resources.

### 4. Coach's Dietary Notes
- **PRACTICE, PRACTICE, PRACTICE**: This is the most important rule. You must use your long runs in August (21k, 24k, 27k) to train your gut and test this exact fueling plan. Find the brand/flavor of gels that work for you. **NOTHING NEW ON RACE DAY.**
- **Hydration is Key**: In the days leading up to the race, focus on hydration. During the race, sip water or sports drink at every opportunity. For a 30k, you will also need to replenish electrolytes, so using a sports drink with sodium is highly recommended.
