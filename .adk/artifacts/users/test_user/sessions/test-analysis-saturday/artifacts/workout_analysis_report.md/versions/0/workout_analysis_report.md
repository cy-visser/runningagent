### 3. Physiological Analysis & Intensity Compliance
- **Intensity Compliance**: Your average heart rate of 147 bpm was in the lower end of your Zone 2, but the run ended with your heart rate in Zone 3 (159 bpm). This indicates a high intensity effort, especially for a runner returning from injury.
- **Pace vs. Heart Rate Efficiency**: Your Pace to Heart Rate ratio (Pa:Hr) was 8.36%, which is quite high. This is known as "aerobic decoupling" and it suggests a significant cardiovascular drift during the run. In simple terms, your heart rate climbed steadily even though your pace was relatively consistent. This is a sign that your cardiovascular system was working much harder to maintain the same effort level as the run progressed.
- **Aerobic Decoupling (Pa:Hr)**: The 8.36% decoupling is a key takeaway from this run. While some decoupling is normal, a value this high when returning from injury suggests that the intensity was likely too high for your current aerobic fitness level.

### 4. Biomechanical & Injury Context
- Your average cadence of 173 steps per minute is a real positive. A higher cadence reduces braking forces and impact on your joints, which is excellent for your calf injury recovery. Keep this up!
- While your cadence is good, the high cardiovascular stress (indicated by the high decoupling) could indirectly put you at further injury risk if your body is not fully recovered.

### 5. Long-Term Goal Progress
- **Goal**: Completing the NYC Marathon on November 1st, 2026
- **Status**: ADJUSTMENTS REQUIRED
- **Coaching Advice**: This was a solid 10k run in terms of distance, and a great step in your return to running. However, to build the strong aerobic base needed for a marathon, the immediate focus should be on controlling the intensity of your runs. For the next few weeks, let's prioritize keeping your heart rate squarely in Zone 1 for the majority of your runs, even if it means slowing down your pace. This will allow your body to adapt and build fitness without undue stress, which is crucial for staying injury-free and on track for your marathon goal.
