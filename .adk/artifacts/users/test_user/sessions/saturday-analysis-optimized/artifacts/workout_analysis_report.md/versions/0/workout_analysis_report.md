### 3. Physiological Analysis & Intensity Compliance
- **Intensity Compliance**: Your average heart rate of 147 bpm was right at the bottom of your Zone 2, which is good. However, your heart rate did climb into Zone 3 for the second half of the run, indicating an increase in effort.
- **Pace vs. Heart Rate Efficiency**: Your Pace to Heart Rate ratio (Pa:Hr) was 8.36%, which is a bit high. This is called 'aerobic decoupling' and it means your heart rate drifted upwards even when your pace didn't increase proportionally. This is very common and expected as you are rebuilding your aerobic base after your calf injury. As your fitness improves, you'll see this number come down.
- **Aerobic Decoupling (Pa:Hr)**: The high decoupling suggests that while your pace was steady, your cardiovascular system had to work progressively harder to maintain it. This is a clear sign that we need to continue focusing on building your aerobic endurance.

### 4. Biomechanical & Injury Context
- Your cadence of 173 steps per minute is solid. Maintaining a higher cadence like this is excellent for reducing impact forces on your joints and is particularly important as you return from your calf injury. This will help you stay healthy as we increase your mileage.
